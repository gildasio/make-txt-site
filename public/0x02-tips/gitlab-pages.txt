---[[ 0x00 Intro ]]-------------------------------------------------------------

Sites made by *Make TXT Site* are simple to be hosted. Gitlab pages is a great
way to do that.

---[[ 0x01 ToC ]]---------------------------------------------------------------

- 0x00 Intro
- 0x01 ToC
- 0x02 Gitlab Pages
- 0x03 Signature

---[[ 0x02 Gitlab Pages ]]------------------------------------------------------

All you need is to create a `.gitlab-ci.yml` file in root directory. A example
content to this file:

```
image: alpine:latest

pages:
  stage: deploy
  script:
  - echo 'Nothing to do...'
  artifacts:
    paths:
    - public
  only:
  - master
```

Maybe you have to change somethings in these file, for example `public` and 
`master` to correspond your posts directory and branch but it isn't likely.

> If your repository is private perhaps you want the pages be public accessible
> anyway. Go to 'Settings > General > Visibility > Pages' and set to 'Everyone'.
